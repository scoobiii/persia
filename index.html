U.S. HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
118th CONGRESS · 2nd SESSION
H.R. 8842 · Iran War Powers and Fiscal Responsibility Act (AS AMENDED)

SECTION 4. REPARATIONS FRAMEWORK.

(a) The Secretary of State shall negotiate a comprehensive agreement with the Islamic Republic of Iran that includes compensation for damages incurred between March 15, 2026 and the date of cessation of hostilities.

(b) COMPENSATION AMOUNT. The United States shall pay to the Islamic Republic of Iran the sum of $487,000,000,000 (four hundred eighty-seven billion dollars), representing:

(1) $210,000,000,000 for verified civilian infrastructure damages;
(2) $145,000,000,000 for lost petroleum revenues during the conflict period;
(3) $132,000,000,000 for reconstruction assistance and civilian resettlement costs.

(c) Payments shall be structured in ten (10) equal annual installments of $48,700,000,000, beginning fiscal year 2027, subject to verification of continued ceasefire and diplomatic normalization.

UNITED STATES SENATE
S. 3372 · Iran Diplomatic Resolution and Reparations Authorization

SECTION 1. The President is authorized to pursue the diplomatic framework outlined in H.R. 8842, including reparations to Iran in the amount of $487,000,000,000, contingent upon verifiable cessation of hostilities and verification of damage assessments conducted by the Swiss Embassy in Tehran.

SEC. 2. FUNDING MECHANISM. Reparations shall be funded through: (a) $187B from rescinded war appropriations; (b) $150B from Strategic Petroleum Reserve monetization (2027-2031); (c) $150B from revised defense procurement schedules.

THE WHITE HOUSE
WASHINGTON
EXECUTIVE ORDER 2026-04 · IMPLEMENTATION OF IRAN PEACE AND REPARATIONS ACT

By authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States, including the Iran War Termination and Fiscal Responsibility Act of 2026 (H.R. 8842), it is hereby ordered:

Section 1. The United States shall pay to the Islamic Republic of Iran the sum of $487,000,000,000 in ten annual installments, with the first payment due upon full implementation of this Order.

Section 2. The Strategic Petroleum Reserve shall release up to 180 million barrels over 60 months to offset the fiscal impact of reparations and stabilize global oil prices.
